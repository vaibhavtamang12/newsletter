Executive Summary

This week was characterized by a sharp de-risking event across global equities, primarily driven by significant profit-taking in high-growth areas and a decisive rotation into defensive, value-oriented assets. While major indices faced pressure, specific 'real-asset' pockets and low-volatility strategies provided positive returns.

Key drivers for the volatility included strategic "Liquidation and profit-taking" as investors prepared for major upcoming IPOs (e.g., SpaceX), alongside a solid US jobs report that bolstered the domestic economic outlook but also signaled that interest rates may remain "higher for longer".

Key Insights & Takeaways
Emerging Markets (EM) Volatility
MSCI EM saw the sharpest weekly decline of −5.85%, closely followed by the Hang Seng (−5.58%). Despite this weekly rout, the EM sector remains a top YTD performer (+14.85%), suggesting a sharp, perhaps necessary, mean-reversion after a very strong 2025 (+34.17%).
Relative Strength in Canada
The S&P/TSX Composite (CAD) was the clear outlier, remaining virtually flat (−0.02%) while all other major indices pulled back significantly. This likely reflects the index's heavy weighting in Energy and Materials, which provided a buffer against the broader market sell-off.
European Weakness (Led by DAX)
The DAX 40 (Germany) dropped −3.04% last week, pushing its YTD performance into negative territory (−1.06%). This highlights ongoing industrial or macroeconomic headwinds in the Eurozone.

Key Insights & Takeaways
Risk-Off Sentiment Dominates
Low Volatility (+1.74%) and Low Vol High Div (+1.13%) were the only positive performers last week. This is a classic "flight to safety" signal, as investors rotated away from aggressive growth into strategies designed to weather market turbulence.
Growth & High Beta Collapse
The most aggressive strategies were the hardest hit. High Beta (−4.54%), Growth (−4.04%), and Pure Growth (−3.91%) led the declines. This suggests a significant deleveraging event in speculative and tech-heavy factors.
Value Resilience
Pure Value (+0.60%) significantly outperformed Value (−0.65%) and Growth. This indicates that the deeper the value discount, the better the protection was during last week's tech-led sell-off.

Key Insights & Takeaways
Defensive Rotation in Full Swing
The market saw a clear shift toward defensive and 'real-asset' sectors. Energy (XLE, +2.45%) and Healthcare (XLV, +2.37%) led the pack, indicating that investors are prioritizing yield and stable cash flows over growth during this volatile period.
Tech Correction is Healthy but Aggressive
Info Tech (XLK) was the worst performer last week, dropping −5.61%. While this looks alarming, it follows a massive 33.64% QTD gain. This suggests profit-taking in high-valuation names rather than a fundamental breakdown of the sector.
Real Estate Resilience
Real Estate (XLRE, +1.61%) continues its strong quarter (+9.16% QTD). This suggests that interest rate expectations may be stabilizing, or investors are seeking inflation-protected assets.
Growth vs. Value Divergence
The sharp underperformance of Consumer Discretionary (XLY, −4.30%) and Communication Services (XLC, −3.47%) alongside Tech confirms that 'Growth' as a factor is currently out of favor compared to 'Value' or 'Defensive' sectors.

The market is currently in a corrective phase for Growth and Tech. As we head into next week, the focus remains on whether the rotation into Value and Defensives (Energy/Healthcare/Low Vol) can continue to provide a floor for the broader market while Tech finds a new support level. The solid jobs data suggests underlying economic strength, providing a fundamental backstop for US markets despite temporary technical liquidations.
